# Apollo Solar Website — Design Brainstorm

## User Requirements
- Theme: Deep Blue (#154E9F) main, Bright Orange (#F7931E) accent
- Remove all shop sections
- Add Key Study and Apollo Projects sections
- Redesign About Us and Philosophy to be more formal and simple
- Overall formal, professional design

## Idea 1: Corporate Blueprint

**Design Movement**: Swiss International / Corporate Modernism
**Core Principles**: Precision grid alignment, restrained color usage, typographic hierarchy as primary visual tool, generous white space
**Color Philosophy**: Deep Blue (#154E9F) dominates as authority and trust. Orange (#F7931E) used sparingly — only for CTAs and key data points. White and Light Gray create breathing room. The palette communicates reliability over excitement.
**Layout Paradigm**: Strict 12-column grid with asymmetric content placement. Left-heavy text blocks with right-aligned supporting visuals. Sections separated by clean horizontal rules rather than background color changes.
**Signature Elements**: (1) Thin orange accent lines that appear at section transitions, (2) Large serif numerals for statistics displayed in a monospaced style
**Interaction Philosophy**: Minimal — subtle opacity transitions on hover, no dramatic animations. Content speaks for itself.
**Animation**: Fade-in on scroll only, 300ms ease. No parallax or complex motion.
**Typography System**: Playfair Display for headings (authority), Inter or DM Sans for body (clarity). Strict size scale: 48/32/24/18/16/14.

## Idea 2: Structured Elegance

**Design Movement**: Neo-Corporate with Editorial influence
**Core Principles**: Clean geometric structure, purposeful contrast between deep blue sections and white sections, data-forward presentation, professional warmth through orange accents
**Color Philosophy**: Deep Blue as the anchor of trust and professionalism. Orange as the "energy" accent — representing solar power itself. Dark Blue (#0D3B7A) for depth in hero and footer. Light Gray (#E5E5E5) as subtle section dividers. Soft Yellow (#FFD966) for subtle glow effects that reference sunlight without being garish.
**Layout Paradigm**: Alternating full-width sections with contained content blocks. Two-column layouts for About/Philosophy with clean dividers. Card-based grids for Projects and Key Studies. Hero uses a bold left-aligned text block with a subtle geometric pattern on the right.
**Signature Elements**: (1) Geometric sun motif — a simple circle with radiating lines used as a recurring brand element, (2) Orange underline accents on section labels, (3) Numbered items with large serif digits
**Interaction Philosophy**: Professional hover states — cards lift with subtle shadow, buttons darken. Smooth scroll between sections. No playful or whimsical interactions.
**Animation**: Scroll-triggered fade-up for content blocks (staggered 100ms). Subtle counter animations for statistics. Clean, professional motion — nothing bouncy.
**Typography System**: Playfair Display (serif) for display headings — conveys establishment and trust. Outfit (sans-serif) for body text — modern and readable. Clear hierarchy with weight variation (700/900 for headings, 300/400 for body).

## Idea 3: Brutalist Corporate

**Design Movement**: Neo-Brutalist Corporate
**Core Principles**: Raw structural honesty, heavy borders, bold type at extreme sizes, high contrast blocks
**Color Philosophy**: Deep Blue as solid block backgrounds. Orange as aggressive highlight — large fills, not just accents. Black borders define every element. White text on blue, dark text on orange.
**Layout Paradigm**: Stacked full-bleed blocks with no rounded corners. Content in rigid boxes with visible 3px borders. Overlapping elements for visual tension.
**Signature Elements**: (1) Thick black borders on all cards, (2) Oversized display type that bleeds off-screen, (3) Rotated text labels
**Interaction Philosophy**: Abrupt state changes — no transitions. Hover states use color inversion.
**Animation**: None. Static presentation. Content loads immediately.
**Typography System**: Space Grotesk at extreme weights. Body in monospace. All caps for labels.
</text>

## Selected Approach: Idea 2 — Structured Elegance

This approach best matches the user's request for a "formal and simple" design while maintaining visual sophistication. The Neo-Corporate with Editorial influence style provides:
- Professional authority through the Deep Blue palette
- Warmth and energy through controlled Orange accents
- Clean, readable layouts that communicate trust
- Enough visual interest through geometric sun motifs and editorial typography without being flashy
